B3 (also known The harmful effects of UV-A and UV-B on the skin are well known, but the skin-ageing effect of blue-violet radiation, which is close to UV-A in terms of wavelength, should not be underestimated either! Indeed, when exposed significantly, it can create free radicals, which can damage membrane lipids and skin proteins. Sunlight literally contains "all the colours of the rainbow", including the aforementioned blue light. Should we fear it immediately? No, not directly, on the contrary, because blue light also helps produce Vitamin D in the skin. However, in recent years there has been an increasing focus on excessive exposure to blue light from screens, such as tablets and smartphones. And whereas with UV radiation the use of "filters" is a successful option, with blue light it is much more difficult and "antioxidants" are the best choice. Prophylactic protection with a powerful, natural antioxidant, such as Glucosyl Rutin (stabilised "Vit. P") is therefore a "must"!
