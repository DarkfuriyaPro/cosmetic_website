Active ingredients:
- Geranium
- The geranium (Pelargonium Graveolens / Odorantissimum) has a wonderful fragrance, is antifungal/antibacterial and also spasmolytic (relaxing).
- Glycerin (Natural Source)
- Vegetable glycerin is a small molecule that attracts and binds water. These characteristics also make it an important ingredient in Natural Moisturizing Factors, which are responsible for maintaining the skin's natural moisture balance.
- At the end of the 18th century, long before the petrochemical industry was established, the German Carl Wilhelm Scheele produced glycerin for the first time in history by saponifying olive oil. Although many changes have been introduced since then, the production process for obtaining vegetable glycerin from natural oil has remained essentially unchanged. Only the purity requirements have been optimized in accordance with the latest pharmaceutical standards. In other words, this means that Medex creates its products with pure vegetable glycerin.
- Lactic acid
- It is an alpha hydroxy acid found in various fruits, such as tomatoes, and in dairy products, such as yogurt. L-Lactic acid is a naturally occurring stereoisomer that also exists in the human body. It has an exfoliating effect when used in higher concentrations. It stimulates the production of new cells in the epidermis. Compared to other alpha hydroxy acids, L-Lactic acid shows the best results in treating skin hyperpigmentation (for example, glycolic acid is much less effective). L-Lactate derivatives (L-Lactic acid) are among the few natural substances known to neutralize the production of the enzyme tyrosinase at high concentrations. Tyrosinase is the enzyme that stimulates skin pigmentation, so by deactivating this enzyme, the first step in pigment production is stopped. Furthermore, L-Lactic acid is responsible for the stereoselective biosynthesis of ceramides in the skin. High levels of ceramides in the epidermis strengthen the lipid barrier, preventing skin dehydration. Interestingly, the stimulation of ceramide biosynthesis only occurs thanks to the natural stereoisomer L-lactic acid.
- Niacinamide
- It is responsible for the synthesis of ceramides in the skin, naturally strengthening the skin barrier by creating lamellar lipid layers. Therefore, it naturally moisturizes the skin. It also improves the skin's defenses. In case of Vitamin B3 deficiency, the skin will develop a very poor appearance after a while (a condition called pellagra).
- Sodium Lactate
- A small, salt-like molecule that binds water to itself and holds it in place. These characteristics also make it an important component of the Natural Moisturizing Factor, responsible for the skin's natural moisture balance.
